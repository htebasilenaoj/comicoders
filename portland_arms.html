SUN, MAY 26TH // THE PORTLAND ARMS



1 PM - The Variables Present: 50 Minutes of
Science Comedy

Following another sell-out run at the Cambridge Science Festival, the Variables bring their unique style to the Cambridge Fringe. Come prepared to laugh out loud as our comedians explore how they got into research, what they spend all day doing, and why they do it at all.

2 PM - Glenn Moore

Edinburgh Comedy Award nominee Glenn Moore (Mock the Week, Absolute Radio and Stand Up Central) presents a work-in-progress new show full of the distinctive jokes and offbeat gags we've come to accept.


3 PM - Rob Kemp: Moonraker 2 - Moonrakerer

Rob Kemp is the multi-award-winning, Edinburgh Fringe Best Newcomer Nominated creator of The Elvis Dead. This is not that. This is a new thing.

4 PM - The Lol Word

The LOL Word are made up of 4 fantastic up-and-coming comedians in their own right. We came together in 2017 because we all felt somewhat marginalised and limited by the comedy scene in London; there didn't seem to be a creative and inclusive space for LGBTQ+ women, non-binary and trans voices.


5 PM - Cally Beaton: Invisible

Invisible is the new show from Cally Beaton (as seen on QI) – an unflinching, hilarious and unexpected insight into life at an age when the world ghosts you. From behind the lines of the mid-life resistance, she gets her shellac-ed claws into survival and mines the funny where you least expect it.

6 PM - Jason Stampe: Bad Guy

Does it make you a bad person to put what makes you happy first? Can you be a bad person and not realise. Do we normalise behaviour that probably isn’t acceptable. How many people have an inner monologue that they wish where brave enough to say out loud?


7 PM - Robyn Perkins: Mating Selection

On August 14, 2018, Robyn Perkins participated in a dating show in front of a live audience. Her experience led her to question human morality and the social constructs of present-day dating, with a scientific twist.

8 PM - Njambi McGrath: Accidental Coconut

It was 2014 when Njambi’s father died. Theirs was a fraught relationship. She decided to let the whole world know what a horrid man he was by writing a book. It was only when she began to research, she discovered that history is only over told from the victor’s side and rarely is the story heard from the other side. This is the other side.


9 PM - Matt Price: Broken Hooters and Geezers
with Shows

Matt Price has been a professional comedian for over ten years and has performed in 30 countries, including 4 tours of Australia. He is a versatile performer who tells a great yarn, usually from the willder side of life, but one that will make you laugh. He is not a geezer and his hooter remains unbroken.

10 PM - Marlon Davis: Emotional Black Male

As seen on Live at the Apollo and Comedy Central's Comedy Store Live, Marlon Davis serves up a brand-new hilarious show. 'Bursts on to the stage like a breath of fresh air, with impish grin, boyish enthusiasm and an effortlessly open, confident style' - Chortle
